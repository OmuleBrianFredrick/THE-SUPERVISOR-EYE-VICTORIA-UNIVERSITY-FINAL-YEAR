# SUPERVISOR EYE ENTERPRISE PLATFORM
## ROLE BREAKDOWN AND DASHBOARD ACTIVITIES

---

### 1. UNDERSTANDING "IMMEDIATE SUBORDINATES"

**What is an Immediate Subordinate?**
In the Supervisor Eye organizational hierarchy, an "immediate subordinate" is an employee who directly reports to a specific user. The hierarchy ensures a clear chain of command for task assignment, report submission, and approval workflows. 
- When a user has a subordinate, it means they are responsible for assigning tasks to that person, reviewing their submitted reports, and monitoring their performance.
- For example, if Christian E. (IT Support & Platform Owner) has a "System Admin" listed as a subordinate, it means the System Admin reports directly to Christian for daily tasks, system maintenance duties, or approvals. Christian acts as their direct manager in the system.

**Why do some have subordinates while others have none?**
- **None (0 Subordinates):** Users at the base of the operational pyramid, such as **Field Staff**, do not manage anyone. Their primary role is to execute tasks, so they have no subordinates.
- **Several (e.g., 5 to 10 Subordinates):** **Supervisors** typically manage a team of Field Staff. **Managers** oversee multiple Supervisors. **Executives** (like the CEO or Operations Director) manage Department Managers and other Executives. The number reflects the actual real-world reporting structure mapped into the platform.

### 2. DETAILED NARRATIVE: ROLES & DASHBOARD ACTIVITIES

The platform dynamically routes users to specific dashboards based on their assigned role. Each dashboard provides tools tailored to the user's responsibilities.

#### A. FIELD STAFF (The Execution Layer)
*Positions: Sales Field Officer, QA Field Representative, Logistics Rep, etc.*
*Dashboard: **Field Staff Dashboard***

**Role Narrative:**
Field Staff are the operational backbone of the organization. They are the personnel on the ground executing daily operations—whether that means visiting clients in Sales, inspecting production lines in QA, or verifying shipments in Logistics. They do not manage others; their focus is entirely on task execution and accurate data collection.

**Dashboard Activities & Actions:**
- **Task Management:** Viewing their daily/weekly assigned tasks from their Supervisor.
- **Report Submission:** Filling out customized report forms based on the task type.
- **Evidence Upload:** Capturing and uploading photographic evidence, documents, or location data to prove task completion.
- **Feedback Handling:** Receiving notifications if a Supervisor rejects a report, reading the feedback, and re-submitting the corrected data.
- **Personal Metrics:** Viewing their own completion rates, pending tasks, and SLA (Service Level Agreement) compliance.

---

#### B. SUPERVISOR (The Verification Layer)
*Positions: Sales Supervisor, QA Division Supervisor, Logistics Supervisor*
*Dashboard: **Supervisor Dashboard***

**Role Narrative:**
Supervisors are responsible for the direct oversight of Field Staff. Their primary duty is quality control. They ensure that the data and evidence submitted by the Field Staff meet the company's standards before it is passed up the chain. They act as the first line of review.

**Dashboard Activities & Actions:**
- **Task Delegation:** Creating and assigning specific tasks to their immediate subordinates (Field Staff), setting deadlines and required evidence.
- **First-Level Review:** Reviewing submitted reports and inspecting uploaded evidence.
- **Approval Workflow:** Approving satisfactory reports (sending them up the chain) or rejecting reports with comments (sending them back to the Field Staff for revision).
- **Team Monitoring:** Tracking the real-time status of their team's tasks, identifying bottlenecks, and monitoring field activity.
- **Escalation:** Escalating critical issues or SLA breaches to the Department Manager.

---

#### C. MANAGER (The Departmental Strategy Layer)
*Positions: Sales Manager, QA Department Manager, Finance Manager*
*Dashboard: **Manager Dashboard***

**Role Narrative:**
Managers oversee entire departments or large divisions. While Supervisors focus on individual tasks, Managers focus on departmental performance and aggregate outcomes. They bridge the gap between executive strategy and field operations, ensuring their department meets its KPIs.

**Dashboard Activities & Actions:**
- **Departmental Analytics:** Viewing aggregated data on departmental performance, task completion rates, and overall efficiency.
- **High-Level Approvals:** Reviewing and approving critical reports that have passed Supervisor scrutiny but require departmental sign-off.
- **Resource Allocation:** Monitoring workload distribution among Supervisors and reallocating resources if certain divisions are overwhelmed.
- **SLA & Compliance Monitoring:** Tracking overdue tasks and SLA breaches across the entire department and applying corrective measures.
- **Reporting to Executives:** Summarizing departmental performance for executive review.

---

#### D. EXECUTIVE & DIRECTORS (The Strategic Oversight Layer)
*Positions: Chief Executive Officer (CEO), Managing Director, Operations Director, IT Director*
*Dashboard: **Executive Dashboard / EACC (Executive Administration & Command Center)***

**Role Narrative:**
Executives possess a macro-view of the entire enterprise. They do not involve themselves in daily task assignments unless absolutely necessary. Instead, they monitor the health of the organization, track cross-departmental efficiency, and make strategic decisions based on data gathered from the ground up.

**Dashboard Activities & Actions:**
- **Enterprise Command Center:** Accessing high-level analytics, financial summaries, and cross-departmental performance metrics in real-time.
- **Global Search & Audit:** The ability to drill down into any specific department, supervisor, or field staff member to audit specific activities.
- **Final Authority Approvals:** Providing final sign-off on major operational reports, financial expenditures, or strategic changes.
- **Risk Management:** Monitoring enterprise-wide SLA breaches, critical alerts, and operational bottlenecks.

---

#### E. SYSTEM ADMIN & SUPER ADMIN (The Technical & Platform Management Layer)
*Positions: IT Developer, IT Support, Platform Owner*
*Dashboard: **Executive Dashboard / EACC***

**Role Narrative:**
The System Admins and IT support personnel ensure the platform operates smoothly. They act as the custodians of the system architecture, managing access, security, and technical configurations.

**Dashboard Activities & Actions:**
- **User Provisioning:** Creating new user accounts, assigning roles, and placing users within the correct organizational hierarchy.
- **System Configuration:** Managing global settings, configuring new report templates, and maintaining data integrity.
- **Technical Support:** Troubleshooting system errors and assisting users who encounter technical difficulties.
- **Audit Logging:** Monitoring system access logs, tracking user activity for security compliance, and maintaining data backups.
