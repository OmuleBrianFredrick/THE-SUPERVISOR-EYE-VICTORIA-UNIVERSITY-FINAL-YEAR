# Business Analysis Report: Movit Group of Companies

## 1. Executive Summary
This report analyzes the business structure, operations, and field challenges of Movit Group of Companies (Movit Products Limited). The analysis acts as the foundational knowledge base for designing **SUPERVISOR EYE**, a hierarchical evidence-based reporting and accountability system tailored specifically for Movit's operational footprint.

## 2. Company Analysis: Movit Group of Companies

### 2.1 Corporate Overview
Movit Products Limited is one of the leading personal care and cosmetics manufacturing companies in Uganda and the broader East African region. Founded in the late 1990s, the company has grown into a fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) powerhouse serving millions of customers across multiple African countries (e.g., Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, DRC, and Zambia).

### 2.2 Products and Services
Movit manufactures and distributes a diverse portfolio of hair care, skin care, and personal care products. Key brands under the Movit umbrella include:
*   **Movit** (Flagship brand: Hair gels, pomades, body lotions, hair relaxers)
*   **Radiant** (Premium hair care products)
*   **Baby Junior** (Baby care products: Soaps, jellies, lotions)
*   **Pine** (Antibacterial soaps and hygiene products)
*   **Nan** (Sanitizers and related products)

### 2.3 Organizational Structure & Departments
Movit operates an extensive, multi-layered organizational structure typical of a large-scale FMCG manufacturer:
*   **Executive Management:** Board of Directors, CEO, C-Suite (COO, CFO, CMO).
*   **Commercial & Sales Division:** Responsible for revenue generation, brand presence, and trade marketing.
*   **Manufacturing & Production:** Factory operations, quality assurance.
*   **Supply Chain & Logistics:** Warehousing, distribution fleet, materials handling.
*   **Human Resources:** Talent management, compliance, performance tracking.
*   **Field Operations:** Territorial sales, merchandising, and brand activations.

### 2.4 Field and Sales Operations
Movit's success heavily relies on its expansive sales and distribution network:
*   **Field Sales Agents & Merchandisers:** Deployed across trade areas, responsible for product placement, visibility, interacting with wholesalers and retailers, and executing BTL (Below The Line) marketing activities.
*   **Van Sales Representatives:** Managing direct-to-retail deliveries.
*   **Regional/Territory Supervisors:** Overseeing smaller groups of agents.
*   **Country/Regional Managers:** Managing broad geographical sales areas and distribution hubs.
*   **Distribution Network:** Wholesalers, sub-distributors, supermarkets, and cosmetic shops.

### 2.5 Branding and Visual Identity
*   **Primary Brand Colors:** Historically associated with Red, White, and Gold depending on the specific brand line (Movit red, Radiant premium gold/black).
*   **Brand Values:** "All Day Confidence," quality, affordability, and widespread accessibility.
*   **Corporate Image:** A proudly African, highly visible, and culturally embedded consumer brand.

## 3. Business Problem Definition

Given the sheer scale of Movit's operations—involving hundreds of field agents moving across vast regional and rural geographies—the company faces several systemic oversight challenges that **SUPERVISOR EYE** aims to solve:

1.  **Employee Reporting Challenges & "Ghost" Reporting:** Remote field staff and merchandisers often struggle to submit timely daily reports. Existing manual or fragmented digital systems (like WhatsApp groups) make it easy to fabricate visits, leading to inaccurate operational visibility.
2.  **Field Staff Supervision & Location Verification:** Supervisors lack real-time visibility into the actual geographic whereabouts of their agents during working hours, leading to inefficiencies and unverified territory coverage.
3.  **Sales Team Monitoring:** Difficulties in tracking actual time spent at retail outlets vs. transit time, and inability to correlate claimed field activity with actual stock movement.
4.  **Performance Tracking:** Relying on subjective end-of-month reviews rather than objective, daily, evidence-backed KPIs for promotions or corrective actions.
5.  **Accountability & Evidence Verification Challenges:** Lack of irrefutable "proof of work." Did the merchandiser actually build the end-cap display? Did the sales agent actually visit the key distributor? Photos sent via chat apps lack metadata (GPS, Timestamp) validation.
6.  **Approval Workflow Bottlenecks:** Paper-based or email-heavy approval structures for expenses, leave, or task completion slow down field agility.
7.  **Management & Executive Reporting:** Managers spend hours consolidating unstructured data from multiple supervisors into Excel sheets. Executives receive delayed, stale data, hindering proactive decision-making.
